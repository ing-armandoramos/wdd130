Culiacan, Sinaloa

Culiacan is the capitol city of the mexican state of Sinaloa, it has population of one million people, it was founded in 1531. Culiacán is known for its culture, luxuries and celebrations. Its iconic sites include its historic center, Las Riberas Park, the Culiacán Botanical Garden, the flagpole known as dancing fountains, the Tomateros stadium and religious temples such as its cathedral basilica, the sanctuary and La Lomita. The Tres Ríos district is known for its buildings, squares, hotels, parks, restaurants and schools. The first layout of the city was carried out by the architect Luis F. Molina.
